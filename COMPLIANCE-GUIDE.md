## CAN-SPAM Act (Email)

### Requirements for Commercial Email

1. **Accurate Headers**
   - Don't use false or misleading "From," "To," or routing information
   - Your domain must accurately identify you

2. **Non-Deceptive Subject Lines**
   - Subject must accurately reflect email content
   - No clickbait or misleading claims

3. **Clear Disclosure**
   - Email must be clearly identified as an advertisement
   - Can be subtle but must be clear

4. **Physical Address**
   - Include your valid physical postal address
   - Can be current street address, PO box, or registered commercial mail drop

5. **Unsubscribe Mechanism**
   - Provide clear, conspicuous explanation of how to opt out
   - Must be able to be processed by any ordinary person
   - Examples: "Reply 'unsubscribe'", link to opt-out page

6. **Honor Opt-Outs Promptly**
   - Process unsubscribe requests within 10 business days
   - Can't charge a fee or require any information beyond email address
   - Can't require recipient to take any step other than visiting one page

### Penalties
- **Up to $53,088 per violating email** (adjusted for inflation 2024)
- **Criminal penalties** for aggravated violations (fraud, hacking, etc.)

### B2B vs B2C
- **B2B (business email addresses):** Lower enforcement priority
- **B2C (personal email addresses):** Higher scrutiny
- **Best practice:** Apply same standards to both

### Template Compliance Checklist
```
✅ Real name and company in signature
✅ Physical mailing address included
✅ Clear unsubscribe option ("Reply 'no thanks' to opt out")
✅ Subject line accurately reflects content
✅ Not misleading about who you are
✅ Honor unsubscribe within 10 days
```

## TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act)

### What's Covered
- **Phone calls** (voice and prerecorded)
- **SMS/Text messages**
- **Fax transmissions**
- **Auto-dialers** (ATDS - Automatic Telephone Dialing Systems)

### Key Rules

#### Calls to Mobile Phones
- **Prior express written consent required** for:
  - Prerecorded/artificial voice calls
  - Auto-dialed calls (including texts)
  - Marketing calls
  
- **Prior express consent (verbal OK)** for:
  - Manual calls (human dials, human speaks)
  - Non-marketing calls

#### Calls to Landlines
- **No auto-dialed prerecorded calls** without prior express consent
- **Manual calls allowed** (but check DNC registry)

### Do Not Call (DNC) Registry
- **Check:** https://www.donotcall.gov
- **Fine:** Up to $50,174 per violation
- **B2B exemption:** Calls between businesses generally exempt
- **Established Business Relationship:** 18 months after last purchase

### SMS/Text Specific Rules
- **Written consent required** for ALL marketing texts
- **Must include:**
  - Clear disclosure that agreeing to receive texts
  - Message frequency disclosure
  - "Msg & data rates may apply"
  - Opt-out instructions (e.g., "Reply STOP to unsubscribe")
- **Can't condition purchase on text consent**

### Penalties
- **$501 to $1,503 per violation** (adjusted 2024)
- **Willful violations:** Up to $4,520 per violation
- **Class action risk:** Statutory damages × number of recipients

### What This Means for Lead Gen
```
✅ Manual calls to business numbers (after checking DNC)
✅ Voicemail drops (if not auto-dialed to mobile)
✅ Research/reference calls

❌ Mass texting business mobile numbers
❌ Auto-dialer calls without consent
❌ Prerecorded calls to mobile without consent
❌ Buying phone lists for mass outreach
```

## Contact Forms

### Legal Status
**✅ Generally legal** — contact forms on business websites are:
- Publicly accessible by design
- Intended for inquiries
- Implied consent for contact

### Best Practices
- **Use for initial contact only**
- **Don't submit repeatedly** (could be harassment)
- **Identify yourself clearly**
- **Include opt-out option**
- **Don't misrepresent purpose**

### Example Contact Form Message
```
Subject: Partnership opportunity

Hi [Company] team,

I was researching [industry] in [location] and came across your business.
[One sentence about what caught your attention].

I help businesses like yours [value proposition]. Would love to explore 
if there's a fit.

Are you the right person to speak with?

Thanks,
[Your Real Name]
[Your Company]
[Your Website]
[Your Email]

---
Not interested? Reply "no thanks" and I won't follow up.
```

## State-Specific Rules

### California (CCPA/CPRA)
- **Consumer data rights** (access, delete, opt-out of sale)
- **B2B exemption** (limited, expires 2023 for some provisions)
- **Penalties:** $2,500-7,500 per violation

### Florida (FCCPA)
- **Stricter than federal** for consumer calls
- **B2B generally exempt**
- **Penalties:** Up to $10,000 per violation

### New York
- **No specific cold email law**
- **General fraud/deception statutes apply**

### Check Your State
Laws vary by state. When in doubt:
1. Check state attorney general website
2. Consult local business attorney
3. Apply strictest standard (California) as default

## International Considerations

### GDPR (EU/UK)
- **Requires lawful basis** for processing personal data
- **Legitimate interest** may apply for B2B
- **Must offer opt-out** in every communication
- **Penalties:** Up to €20M or 4% global revenue

### CASL (Canada)
- **Requires express or implied consent** for commercial email
- **Very strict** — fines up to CAD $10M
- **Avoid Canadian recipients** unless you have consent

### Best Practice
**For US-focused lead gen:**
- Stick to US businesses only
- Clearly identify US location in emails
- Avoid EU/UK/Canada unless you have explicit consent

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

### Low-Risk Approach
1. **Focus on B2B only** (business email addresses, business phone numbers)
2. **Use contact forms** for initial outreach when possible
3. **Email preferred over phone** (lower risk profile)
4. **Manual processes** over automation (no auto-dialers)
5. **Clear opt-out** in every message
6. **Honor opt-outs immediately**

### Documentation
Keep records of:
- **When** you contacted each lead
- **What** message you sent
- **Opt-out requests** and when honored
- **Source** of contact information (public website, etc.)

### Red Flags to Avoid
- ❌ Buying email/phone lists from third parties
- ❌ Scraping personal email addresses (Gmail, Yahoo, etc.)
- ❌ Using auto-dialers or mass texting platforms
- ❌ Hiding your identity or business purpose
- ❌ Ignoring opt-out requests
- ❌ Misleading subject lines or claims
- ❌ Contacting after explicit "do not contact" request

## Email Template Compliance Check

### ✅ Compliant Template
```
Subject: Quick question about [Company]'s [specific thing]

Hi [Name],

[Personalized opening about their business]

[Value proposition - what you do]

[Call to action - low pressure]

Best,
[Your Real Name]
[Your Company]
[Your Website]
[Your Phone Number]
[Your Physical Address or "Based in [City, State]"]

---
Not interested? Reply "no thanks" and I won't follow up.
```

### ❌ Non-Compliant Template
```
Subject: RE: Your inquiry  ← Deceptive (not a reply)
From: "Google Partner" ← Misleading identity

Hey,

I can help you get more customers!!! ← Vague, no identity

Click here to learn more → ← No unsubscribe

[No signature, no address]
```

## Compliance Checklist

### Before Sending
- [ ] Verified recipient is a business (not consumer)
- [ ] Email/phone obtained from public business source
- [ ] Checked DNC registry (for calls)
- [ ] Email template includes real identity
- [ ] Email template includes physical address
- [ ] Email template includes unsubscribe option
- [ ] Subject line is accurate and non-deceptive
- [ ] No misleading claims in message

### After Sending
- [ ] Tracking opt-out requests
- [ ] Honoring unsubscribes within 10 days
- [ ] Not contacting after opt-out
- [ ] Maintaining records of communications

### Monthly Review
- [ ] Review bounce rates (high = list quality issue)
- [ ] Review spam complaints (any = template issue)
- [ ] Review opt-out rate (normal: 1-5%, high: reevaluate approach)
- [ ] Update templates if needed

## When to Consult an Attorney

**Consult a lawyer if:**
- Planning large-scale campaign (10,000+ contacts)
- Targeting consumers (B2C) instead of businesses
- Using auto-dialers or mass texting
- Operating in multiple states/countries
- Received cease & desist or complaint
- Unsure about specific situation

**Find a lawyer:**
- State bar association referral service
- Small business legal clinics
- Online: Avvo, FindLaw, Justia

## Summary

### ✅ Safe Zone (Low Risk)
- B2B cold email with CAN-SPAM compliance
- Contact form submissions
- Manual calls to business landlines (check DNC)
- Personalized, honest outreach

### ⚠️ Gray Zone (Medium Risk)
- Calls to business mobile numbers
- High-volume email (1000+/day)
- B2C email (consumers vs businesses)
- International outreach

### ❌ Danger Zone (High Risk)
- Mass texting without consent
- Auto-dialer calls to mobile
- Buying random contact lists
- Hiding identity or misleading
- Ignoring opt-out requests
